The History of Sacred Heart Catholic Church

The history and beginning of Bhayandar Syro-Malabar Community started in 1980, when small gatherings of around 30 families who settled In Bhayandar, Mira Road and Kashimira areas, began coming together for meeting their spiritual and social needs. The community became active with the support from Our Lady Of Nazareth Church, Bhayandar West.
On 28th September, 1984 Rev. Fr. Chelsius C.M.I. Celebrated a Malayalam Qurbana In the Syro-Malabar Rite for the community at Our Lady Of Nazareth Church. In 1985, Malayalam Qurbana was started on every second Sunday and this continued till 1988.The Keralite priests from Shalini Bhavan, Sion and K.C.A. Bhavan, Kalina, Santhacruz came for administering the Holy Eucharist and the Liturgical needs of the Community. In 1988, the size of the Syro-Malabar community of Bhayandar grew to 80 families. On 1st September, 1988 the Bhayander Sacred Heart Church was formed. Rev. Fr. Varghese Nadackal was the first vicar of the Church.

On 1st August, 1990 the community began the efforts to build own prayer hall under the leadership of the then Vicar Rev. Fr. Jose Ainickal. In 1992, with the mercy of God Almighty and with the help of well-wishers and under the leadership of Rev. Fr. Davis Pullikkottill and Our Lady Of Nazareth Church authorities, the community could buy a plot of 13 guntas In Bhyandar East. On April 30th, 1992 His Excellency Mar Paul Chittilapilly the First Bishop of Kalyan diocese laid the foundation stone for the Bhayander Sacred Heart Catholic Welfare Centre. At this time the parish was divided into four zones and further divided Into 15 family units. In 1997, Rev. Fr. Joseph Pothenparampil, M.S.T. took charge as the Parish Priest. A Committee of 64 members was formed for the construction of the Church and started the work on 31st of May, 1999 with the blessings of,. Rev. Fr. Antony Kollannur, the then Chancellor of Kalyan Eparchy. Thus on 17th of February, 2001 the dream of Bhayandar Syro-Malabar Catholics was fulfilled when His Excellency M ana, consecrated the Sacred Heart Church. Bhayandar. On, the same day the Bhayandar Sacred Heart Welfare Centre was also inaugurated by Mr Praful Kashinath Patil, the chairman of Mira Bhayandar Muncipal Council. Former parish priests who served the parish after the church was built are Rev. Fr. Joju Arackal, Rev. Fr. Jose Kokkopuzha, Rev. Fr. Davis Chalissery, Rev. Fr Martin Kombara, Fr. Dennis Maroky, Fr. Joemon Kurisingal, Fr. Jitto Thengumpallil and Fr. Anoop Poyyaniyil. Presently Rev. Fr. Thomas Joseph leads the Parish and takes care of the spiritual needs of the parishioners and coordinates the administration and management of the church activities.

Sacred Heart Catholic Trust was formed in 2009 to co-ordinate the various activities of the Church. Sacred Heart Church, actively contributes to the charity works of Karunya Trust -the Social Service unit of the Diocese and reaches the needy people through educational, medical and other supports. Since 2008, Congregation of the Daughters of Divine Providence provides invaluable service to the pansh and its members through liturgical and venous youth and family development activities. Sacred Heart Church was officially erected as a parish on 8th September 2010 and Msgr. Jacob Porathur handed over the official documents to Rev. Fr. Dennis Maroky during the solemn celebration of Holy Eucharist. In 2011, Sacred Heart Church Bhayandar celebrated its 10th Anniversary of the construction of our Church. In 2013, the Syro-Malabar Community celebrated the Jubilee year marking the completion of its 25 Years of existence in Bhayandar. Currently, the parish has 270 families as active members of the parish.
